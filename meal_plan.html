MEAL PLAN

Good nutrition should taste great and make you feel even better!

However, when it comes to planning and preparing healthy meals and snacks, it can sometimes seem overwhelming, so having a bit of structure and guidance may be just what you need. With this in mind, we worked with registered dieticians to create the Fitness Vision Meal Plan, which highlights great-tasting, healthy, whole-foods meals and snacks - giving you everything you need to make some real headway in learning how to fuel your body better. The plan details 3 meals and 3 snacks - every day - for four weeks.

Sample Nutrition Plan

Caloric Intake

Total Daily Calories: Around 2500-3000 calories (adjust based on individual needs, goals, and activity level)

Macronutrient Distribution

  • Protein: 20-30% of total calories
  • Carbohydrates: 45-65% of total calories
  • Fats: 20-35% of total calories

Meal Plan

  • Breakfast (Around 500-600 calories):
    • 2 whole eggs and 2 egg whites scrambled with spinach and bell peppers
    • 1 slice of whole-grain toast
    • 1 medium-sized fruit (e.g., apple, banana, or orange)
    • 1 cup of low-fat Greek yogurt or cottage cheese
  • Mid-Morning Snack (Around 200-300 calories):
    • Handful of mixed nuts (almonds, walnuts, or pistachios)
    • 1 small piece of fruit (e.g., apple or pear)
  • Lunch (Around 600-700 calories):
    • Grilled chicken breast or tofu
    • Quinoa or brown rice
    • Steamed vegetables (broccoli, carrots, and bell peppers)
    • Side salad with mixed greens, tomatoes, cucumbers, and balsamic vinaigrette dressing
  • Afternoon Snack (Around 200-300 calories):
    • Protein shake made with whey protein powder and almond milk
    • 1 medium-sized fruit (e.g., berries or grapes)
  • Dinner (Around 600-700 calories):
    • Grilled salmon or lean beef
    • Sweet potatoes or whole grain pasta
    • Saut√©ed or roasted vegetables (asparagus, zucchini, and Brussels sprouts)
    • Side salad with mixed greens, avocado, and olive oil dressing
  • Evening Snack (Optional, Around 150-200 calories):
    • Greek yogurt with a drizzle of honey
    • 1 serving of whole-grain crackers with hummus

Importance of Each Nutrient

  • Protein: Essential for muscle repair and growth. Helps to keep you feeling full and satisfied.
  • Carbohydrates: Main source of energy for your body and brain. Choose complex carbohydrates for sustained energy levels.
  • Fats: Important for hormone production, brain function, and absorption of fat-soluble vitamins. Opt for healthy fats like those found in nuts, seeds, avocados, and fatty fish.

Hydration

Drink plenty of water throughout the day to stay hydrated, especially during and after workouts.

Important Notes:

  • This is a general nutrition plan and may need to be adjusted based on individual needs, preferences, and goals.
  • Consider consulting with our registered dietitian or nutritionist for personalized guidance and recommendations.
